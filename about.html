Get to Know Me

I'm Valerio Solinas, originally from the stunning island of Sardinia, Italy.
I studied at ITI G.M. Angioy in Sassari, where I earned a diploma in Electrical Engineering and Automation/Electronics.
Outside the screen, I'm passionate about football, travel, and anything tech or physics related.
I enjoy growing my knowledge on anything, simple or complicated, it doesn't really matter.
I loved helping my father when he was working as a builder and learning all I could about it.
I loved helping my mother in her coffee shop and seeing what it means to take care not only of tasks but also of people.
My passion for tech started when I was very young.
With the help of my uncle, I still remember touching, for the first time, the power button on an HP Vectra with Windows 95.
He opened an editor to create cartoonish pictures and printed my first creation.
For me, that was THE moment!

My work history

My work life is just a long adventure:
In Italy, I did many different roles.
I started with leafleting from a very young age to financially help my family.
I worked in food delivery for a small restaurant and then worked inside the same restaurant.
From this restaurant, I developed a passion for learning and acquiring skills. There, I started by folding pizza boxes until I became a pizza chef.
Wanting more, I jumped from hospitality to an office job (customer help center), where my task was to help or fix issues that people were encountering during their journey with the company.
Fortunately for me, the experience I gained from those different jobs gave me even more drive to expand my knowledge.
The next step was to see the world from a different perspective, so I decided to move to London.
It was a bit hard to change cities and get out of my comfort zone, everything was different, and my English level at that time was not the best.
I started as a kitchen porter in a pub; this job gave me the chance to improve my spoken and written English.
Hard work always pays off, and from a porter, I became a commis chef and so on, until I was in charge of the kitchen and all the responsibilities that came with it.
From there, I moved on to become a kitchen supervisor, a kitchen trainer, and eventually into a general supervising role.
Not wanting to stop, I became an assistant manager, where I learned what it means to have responsibility for large teams.
As an assistant manager, I learned more about the business side and administration.
I currently work as a supervisor in a bakery. I stepped down from assistant manager to focus on my growth as a developer.
These roles have taught me a lot about responsibility, accountability, efficiency, and connecting with people.
At the heart of it, I'm someone who enjoys both structure and creativity, and I'm always looking to grow as a developer and as a person.
Not wanting to bind myself to a managerial role (the reason being that a managerial role in hospitality was not my dream or goal, and also I couldn't fully focus on my personal growth),
I started getting back to my passion, which is tech-related. I started studying with Code Institute, and under the guidance of mentors and a tutoring system, I earned my Diploma in Advanced Front-End.
