Get to Know Me

I'm Valerio Solinas, originally from the stunning island of Sardinia, Italy.
I studied at ITI G.M. Angioy in Sassari, where I earned a diploma in Electrical Engineering and Automation/Electronics.
Outside the screen, I'm passionate about football, travel, and anything tech or physics related.
I enjoy to grow my knowledge on anything, simple of complicated doesn't really matter.
I loved helping my father when he was working as a builder and learning all I could about it.
I loved helping my mother in her Coffee shop and see what it means to take care not only about tasks but also about people.
My passion for tech started when I was very young,
with the help of my uncle I still remember touching for the first time, the power button on a HP Vectra with Windows 95.
He opened an editor to create cartoonish pictures and printed my first creation.
For me that was the moment that I realized that I loved designing and how tech would help me to do it.

My work history

My work life is just a long adventure:
In Italy I did many different roles.
I started from leafleting from a very young age, to financially help my family.
I worked in food delivery in a small restaurant and then worked in the same restaurant.
From this restaurant I started to get the passion to learn more and aquire skills, there I started folding pizza boxes until I became a pizza chef.
Wanting more I jumped from hospitality to an office job (Customer help center) where my task was to help or fix issue that people were encountering during their journey with that company.
Fortunately for me, the experience that I got from those different jobs gave me more drive to expand my knowledge.
The next step was to see the world from a different prospective, so I decided to move to London.
It was a bit hard to change city and get out of my comfort zone, everything was different and my english level at that period was not the best.
I started as kitchen porter in a pub, this job gave me the chance to improve my spoken and written english.
Hard work always pays off, and from a porter I became a commis chef and so on until I was in charge of the kitchen and all the responsability that came with that.
From there I changed to be a kitchen supervisor , a kitchen trainer and moved to a general supervising role.
Not wanting to stop I became an assistant manager. Where I learned what it means to have responsability for large teams (I was in charge of 35/40 people.).
As an assistant manager I learned more about the business side and administration.
I currently work as a supervisor in a bakery. Step down from assistant to focus on my growth as a developer.
These roles have taught me a lot about responsibility, efficiency, and connecting with people , qualities I bring into every project I build.
At the heart of it, I'm someone who enjoys both structure and creativity, and I'm always looking to grow as a developer and as a person.
Not wanting to bind myself to a manager role (The reason is that a manager role in hospitality was not my dream or goal and also I couldn't completly focus on my personal growth.)
I started getting back on my passion, that is tech related. I started studying with Code Institute , under the guidance of Mentors and a tutoring system I earned my Diploma In advanced front-end.
